The Disconnect
Audit™

A 10–15 minute self-assessment to identify where revenue already exists inside your business — and where disconnection is quietly costing you.

How to use this audit: This is not a mindset exercise. It is not a planning document. It is designed to help you see clearly — where revenue exists, what has momentum but no follow-through, and what can be reactivated without creating anything new. Be honest. Do not optimize your answers.

Section 01

Past Proposals and Quotes

Review the last 6–12 months. Check every statement that is true for you.

  • I have sent proposals that were never formally closed
  • I can name at least 3 proposals that stalled due to timing, not fit
  • I followed up fewer than 3 times on most proposals
  • I assumed silence meant no — without seeking confirmation
  • I revised the offer instead of reopening the conversation

Check the statements that are true for you above.
Section 02

Warm Conversations That Went Quiet

Think about conversations that ended with: "Let's revisit this" / "Circle back soon" / "Not right now."

  • I can name at least 5 people who expressed real interest but did not convert
  • I did not follow up after the initial pause
  • Re-initiating the conversation felt uncomfortable, so I didn't
  • I waited for them to reach back out rather than returning myself
  • I mentally labeled the opportunity as dead without confirmation

Check the statements that are true for you above.
Section 03

Existing Relationships and Network

This includes former clients, collaborators, and referrals who expressed interest but never converted.

  • Past clients had strong outcomes but I have never re-engaged them
  • I rely on new visibility rather than activating existing relationships
  • I don't have a clear or natural reason to re-enter past conversations
  • I underestimate the goodwill that already exists in my network
  • I rarely initiate reconnection unless there is something specific to sell

Section 04

Offers With Proof but No Momentum

Look at what has already worked. Be honest about what you stopped doing — and why.

  • I have an offer that has converted before and is no longer being actively positioned
  • I stopped promoting it after a quiet period rather than examining the follow-through
  • I changed the positioning instead of improving the execution
  • I assume the offer is outdated without evidence to support that
  • I am more comfortable building something new than finishing what already has traction

Section 05

Your Client Experience Gaps

This section is about what happens inside your client relationships — not just how you acquire them.

  • I do not have a defined consultation process that creates a consistent first impression
  • My client touchpoints are transactional — they happen at billing or delivery, not in between
  • I do not have a deliberate process for what happens after an engagement closes
  • I cannot clearly articulate what makes a client choose to stay, refer, or return
  • The experience I deliver has never been intentionally designed — it evolved by default

Section 06

Your Decision Pattern

Be honest. This section is not about what you intend — it is about what you actually do.

  • Starting something new feels lighter than returning to something unfinished
  • I associate reactivation with pressure or the risk of rejection
  • I delay follow-up because I tell myself I am being respectful
  • I equate silence with disinterest — and rarely test that assumption
  • I would rather redesign than re-engage

Interpreting Your Results

recognition — or resistance.

You do not need to calculate a score. Use the descriptions below to orient yourself to where your revenue is actually sitting.

Unfinished, Still Alive
Several sections surfaced work that came close. Proposals sent. Interest expressed. Conversations that had real momentum. The quiet that followed was never a closed door — it was an open one that no one walked back through. That changes now.
Warm but Untouched
The sections about paused conversations and follow-up avoidance carried the most weight. The interest was real on both sides. Re-engagement felt uncomfortable. Silence filled the space — and over time, silence started to feel like an answer. It is not. These relationships are still reachable.
Avoiding Return
The pattern showed up clearly: the pull toward something new is consistently stronger than the return to something already in motion. There is no shortage of opportunity here. There is a habit of moving away from it — toward the familiar relief of starting fresh. Recognizing the habit is the beginning of changing it.
All three Revenue Realities share the same truth: the revenue is not blocked. It is waiting. Seeing the pattern clearly is the first step. Knowing how to move through it — with precision, not pressure — is the second.
