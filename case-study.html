01   The Situation

A full client list.
A revenue problem
nobody had named.

The practice had been open for years. Full client database. Premium services. A reputation that commanded real pricing in their market.

Underneath that: the pricing didn't reflect the work. The service structure had drifted from what their best clients actually needed. And the database, while large, was full of relationships that no longer fit the direction the practice was heading.

The business wasn't underperforming because it lacked clients. It was underperforming because it had never gotten specific about which clients it was actually built for.

Nobody had gone back for the ones worth keeping.

02   The Problem

Three things were true
at the same time.

i
The pricing was below the value being delivered.
ii
The service structure wasn't designed around the client the practice served best.
iii
Sitting inside that database were quiet relationships. Clients who had stopped coming back, not because they'd moved on, but because nothing had pulled them back.

That's recoverable distance. The engagement was built around closing it.

03   The Engagement · 90 Days

Three campaign layers.
Run simultaneously.

Layer A
The full contact list. A sequenced campaign written in the practice's voice. Not a promotion. A genuine reconnection that reintroduced the practice on its terms, with clarity about what it now offered and who it was built for.
Layer B
The highest-value quiet relationships. Handled directly by the lead strategist. Personal, calibrated outreach. Not a template. Not a sequence. Direct contact, specific to each relationship, written and sent personally.
Layer C
Contacts who showed interest and went quiet. A softer entry. A value touchpoint first. A direct invitation second.

Before a single message went out, the database was segmented. The relationships worth recovering were identified and tiered by depth and recoverable value. The ones that no longer fit were wrapped up with care.

The Encore Experience™

Every quiet client who responded and booked back in received a curated welcome-back moment. Preferences captured in advance. A considered gesture ready on arrival. A rebooking conversation built into checkout. Clients didn't just come back. They came back into something better than what they left.

Parallel to the campaign, LinesinTandem™ restructured the service offering. Pricing realigned to reflect the actual value delivered. Services rebuilt around what the right client needed, not what the original model had defaulted to. By the time reactivated clients walked back in, the practice was ready to receive them.
